Lane Detection for Autonomous Vehicles

January 2023 - May 2023

  • Developed a lane detection system designed to enhance navigation in autonomous vehicles using Python, OpenCV, and Machine Learning.
  • Implemented a lane detection algorithm utilizing Sobel edge detection, sliding window search, and polynomial curve fitting, adapting effectively to various lane markings and reducing computational demand.
  • Successfully evaluated and optimized the algorithm to achieve real-time performance, demonstrating up to 25.6 frames per second across different lane types on a moderate computational setup.
  • Demonstrated robustness in diverse driving conditions, efficiently balancing accuracy and computational complexity, and visually rendering lanes on original images to support advanced driver assistance systems.
  • Addressed challenges in detecting discontinuous or missing lane markings and accurately identifying lanes on curved roads, with plans to integrate additional sensors like lidar or infrared to enhance low-visibility conditions performance.
  • Applied advanced computer vision techniques such as perspective transformation and color thresholding to improve the accuracy of lane boundary identification.
  • Integrated sophisticated image processing workflows to handle real-time video streams effectively, crucial for autonomous vehicle systems deployment.
  • Outcome of the project: Successfully integrated with a simulated autonomous driving system to detect and track road lanes in real time under various lighting conditions.
  • Planned future enhancements include adopting deep learning models to improve the detection of complex lane markings and expand capabilities in challenging driving scenarios such as intersections and heavy traffic.
  • Challenges faced during the project: Overcoming issues with detecting lanes under low light conditions and distinguishing between different types of lane markings.
